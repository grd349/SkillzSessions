Intro

Skills sessions are designed to to transfer skills and knowledge into and around our group. Sessions are organised by the presenter but should be accessible, useful and complete. Skills sessions can tackle any topic that they think would be useful to the research group but the sessions will mainly focus on skills for early stage researchers.

Skills sessions have been run over a number of years now and last year we took feedback on the sorts of topics that the group would like to see and how they would like the sessions delivered. In order to start the skills sessions in the right direction we have produced a provisional schedule of skills sessions following the feedback of last year.

  • Giving good talks
  • Using BlueBEAR or the Sims machines (inc RDS)
  • Project Management
  • Good Git workflow
  • Jupyter notebooks
  • Usefull Python libraries
  • Bayesian Statistics in less than 40 hours

Again following feedback from last year, we will attempt to give sessions a degree of permanence. Resources will be stored in accessible areas. Sessions should build on previous sessions and sessions should not be too long (max 1.5 hours). While it might be unrealisitic to achieve expert status on this time scale, sessions should work to break down barriers to entry.
